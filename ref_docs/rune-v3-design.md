# Rune v3.0.0 — Design Document

**Two-Level Type Theory · A Second Equality Stratum** · *a delta on [v1](./rune-v1-design.md) and [v2](./rune-v2-design.md)*

> **Release criterion (and a deliberate honesty about it).** v3.0.0 ships when the v3 chapters of *Specify & Verify* — the ones that reason in the inner, fibrant layer, including a univalent construction — elaborate and check. Note "check," not "run": the honest v3 deliverable is principled *coexistence* with inner univalence available to **reason with**, not yet to **compute with**. Computational inner univalence is the labelled frontier in §F.

*This is the hard version of the roadmap, and the one that vindicates the whole thesis. v1 built the equality interface; v2 proved it extensible; v3 proves it **swappable-at-a-cost** by putting a second equality behind it. It is a different core, reusing the orthogonal substrate — and it is a research vehicle, told straight. Where v1 and v2 were "this will ship," v3 is "this is the frontier; here is exactly what is real now and what is research." That honesty is not a hedge; for this audience it is the only credible register.*

---

## Lightbulb 1 — Where you're standing now (you have quotients, and an itch)
You shipped v2. You have `ℤ` and multisets and your discourse-structures-modulo-R as first-class objects, and you made peace with no univalence. And it still itches — because there is *one* project where you want it. A univalent construction. Transport across an equivalence as a programming idiom. A corner of synthetic homotopy that would make a theorem you care about fall out in a line. You are not locked out because univalence is useless to you. You are locked out **globally, for a thing you need locally** — and that asymmetry is the tell that the architecture, not the mathematics, is now the thing in your way.

## Lightbulb 2 — What you've tried
You made peace: it works, the itch remains. You forked to Cubical Agda for that one project — and now you've left your substrate behind, your content-addressed store, your codegen, your QTT, your whole way of working, in exile for a single construction. Or you tried to bolt univalence onto the OTT core directly, and proved to yourself that you can't: the proof-irrelevant equality validates UIP, `ua(not) : Bool = Bool` is provably distinct from `refl`, UIP says it isn't — they contradict, and no amount of cleverness reconciles two incompatible notions inside *one* equality.

## Lightbulb 3 — The idea
**You don't choose UIP-or-univalence globally. You stratify.** Keep two equalities, side by side, each in its own layer: a strict outer equality satisfying UIP — your OTT equality, doing the metatheoretic and definitional work, the fast content-addressed core you already have — and an inner, *fibrant* equality where univalence is allowed to live. The two never contradict, because they are never the same relation; the strict layer reasons *about* the fibrant layer.

This is not a foreign import. It is **the substrate's own thesis applied to equality itself.** You already put names only in the layer that reads, quantities behind a parameter, targets behind a menu. Two-level type theory (Annenkov–Capriotti–Kraus–Sattler) is the same move — the right notion of sameness in the right layer — made at the one place everyone told you it couldn't be made. That is why v3 is the vindication and not merely the sequel.

And the honest half of the idea, stated before the offer rather than buried under it: 2LTT gives you principled coexistence and lets you **postulate** inner univalence and reason with it soundly. Making inner univalence **compute** is a separate, harder thing that routes back to cubical machinery for the inner layer. v3 ships the first. The second is §F.

## Lightbulb 4 — What you get: Rune v3.0.0 (the delta)

**The second equality stratum.** Alongside the strict `Eq` (unchanged from v1), an inner identity type living in a universe of **fibrant** types. A fibrancy discipline: a judgment or predicate marking which types are inner/fibrant, with the strict layer able to quantify over and reason about them. Inner univalence and the genuinely higher inductive types (the circle, suspensions, higher truncations — everything v2 correctly refused) become expressible **in the inner layer**, postulated where they don't yet compute, with the strict outer layer carrying every proof that needs UIP.

**What this buys structurally:** the homotopy lives in a quarantine. The outer layer stays exactly the tractable, fast, content-addressed OTT core — definitional proof irrelevance, glued NbE, the K-friendly metatheory — because none of that changed. The thing you fled to Cubical Agda *for* now has a home that doesn't cost you the home you built.

**What transfers, untouched** — and this is why v3 is a swap and not a from-scratch rewrite. The content-addressed store and hashing discipline (the syntax grows with inner formers; the rule "hash by syntax, never modulo conversion" is identical). The parametric QTT semiring. Codegen-as-plugin and the erasure boundary. The surface/nameless three-representation split and the Pitts theorem that forces it — **binding is orthogonal to equality**, so the entire §3-of-v1 apparatus is reused verbatim. The Iron Sieve harness discipline. The book-as-freeze. Everything that made Rune *Rune* rather than a type theory is orthogonal to which equalities it has.

**What changes** — the honest scope of the swap. You add the second identity type and the fibrancy layer, and the evaluator and conversion must now operate at two levels and respect fibrancy. The cache-soundness seam gets *more* subtle, because conversion may now consult the inner layer too, so the definitional-closure key has to capture inner-layer unfolding as well as outer (§Risks). And the elaborator must track which layer each judgment lives in.

**§F — The frontier (labelled, not sold).** To make inner univalence *compute* rather than merely be postulated, the inner layer must become **cubical**: an interval, Kan operations, `Glue`. This is the exact research-grade machinery v1 fled — but the stratification changes its character completely. It is no longer infecting the whole core; it is **isolated to the inner stratum**, behind the equality interface, with the outer core and the entire orthogonal substrate untouched. That is the strongest possible vindication of the strata thesis: even the thing you most wanted to avoid, when it finally comes, comes *contained*. A cubical-inner Rune with a content-addressed store is, to my knowledge, unexplored territory — which makes §F not a checkbox but a genuine research programme, and plausibly the technical heart of a thesis. **XTT** (Sterling–Angiuli–Gratzer) will surface in your reading here and is *not* the door: it uses cubical technology to deliver OTT-style guarantees with decidable conversion, but it keeps UIP — an alternative implementation of what you already chose, not a route to univalence.

## Lightbulb 5 — Your new life
One substrate, two equalities. The univalent project lives in the inner layer without exiling you from your store, your codegen, your quantities, or your way of working. The architecture's whole philosophy is finally complete and visible end to end: names where you read, quantities as a parameter, targets as a menu, and now **equality as a stratum** — the right notion of sameness in the right layer, even for the notion everyone said you'd have to leave home to get. And because the cubical-inner frontier on a content-addressed substrate is unexplored, v3 is not only your tool reaching its final form; it is the research vehicle, and the thing the hardest chapters of the book are *about*.

## Non-Goals (v3.0.0)
Not, in the shipping release, *computational* inner univalence — that is §F, the frontier, and conflating the two would be the one dishonesty this whole document exists to avoid. Not a replacement of the outer OTT core — the strict layer is load-bearing and stays. Not XTT (keeps UIP; not a path to univalence). Not a general claim that you should reach for the inner layer often — most work stays in the outer core where it's fast and cached; the inner layer is for the constructions that genuinely need it.

## Risks and Mitigations
- **This is a different core, and the largest undertaking of the three.** *Mitigation:* the orthogonal substrate is reused, not rebuilt; the strict layer is the v1 core unchanged; and the book-as-freeze caps the inner layer to exactly the constructions the v3 chapters demonstrate.
- **Cache soundness across two layers.** Conversion consulting the inner layer means the definitional-closure key must capture inner unfolding too, or proofs cached against an inner definition could go stale. *Mitigation:* the sound full-closure fallback degrades safely; the precise two-layer tracking is the harder version of the v1 seam, property-stated and mutation-tested — and is itself chapter-worthy.
- **The frontier is genuinely open.** Cubical-inner-plus-content-addressing has no reference implementation. *Mitigation:* v3 ships with univalence *postulated* (sound, reasoned-with) and treats §F as research, not release; the postulated version is useful on its own and does not block on the frontier.
- **The discipline failure mode, at maximum intensity.** This is the single most seductive foundational rabbit hole in the field, and it is now *inside* your roadmap. *Mitigation:* the parking lot and chapter gates matter most here; the rule "the inner layer contains exactly what the v3 chapters teach" is the only thing standing between a vindicated thesis and an unfinished one.
